About Me

I am pursuing my M.Tech in Communication and Signal Processing at IIT Indore, having completed my B.Tech in Electronics and Communication Engineering at BTKIT Dwarahat.

My research focuses on Quantum Communication and QKD, applying Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL) to optimize quantum channel protocols and key distribution strategies. This sits on a foundation of Statistical Signal Processing, Optimization, and Estimation Theory, alongside work in Digital Logic, Information Theory, and Coding Theory.

I am drawn to modern communication paradigms — Software Defined Networking (SDN) and Cognitive Radio Networks (CRN) — and actively apply ML and Deep Learning to signal processing problems. Outside engineering, I find balance through poetry and philosophy.

IEEE Publication

Enhancing Two-Area Interconnected Power Systems Using Model Predictive and PID Control

Comparative study of Model Predictive Control (MPC) vs. PID strategies for load frequency control and disturbance rejection in multi-area interconnected power systems. Demonstrates superior transient response and stability margins of MPC under realistic load variation, with quantified improvement in frequency deviation and settling time.

Modulation Classification via Deep Learning

Automated identification of radio modulation schemes using CNN and LSTM networks trained on RF datasets. Classifies BPSK, QPSK, QAM, FSK — enabling adaptive receivers for cognitive radio and next-generation wireless systems.

02 / DSP + CRYPTOGRAPHY

Secure Image Encryption via DWT & Chaotic Maps

Symmetric-key image encryption using a permutation-diffusion architecture. DWT de-correlates pixel data; a chaotic logistic map drives scrambling and XOR-based diffusion. Achieves lossless reconstruction (MSE = 0, PSNR = ∞) with a perfectly uniform encrypted histogram — resistant to statistical attacks.
